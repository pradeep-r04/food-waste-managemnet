About Daawat

Daawat is more than just an app‚Äîit's a community movement aiming to eliminate food wastage and hunger by connecting people who have excess food with those who need it the most. With a simple hosting and attending mechanism, Daawat empowers individuals to make a meaningful difference in their neighborhoods, one meal at a time.

üìå How We Are Helping

Daawat creates an ecosystem where surplus food can be rescued and redirected before it is wasted. Our key efforts include:

  • Peer-to-peer food sharing: Anyone with extra food can become a host and make it available for pickup or dining.
  • Real-time food availability: Users can view nearby "Daawats" (food events) in real time and attend them before the food goes to waste.
  • Hyperlocal impact: By focusing on nearby users, we ensure fast and accessible redistribution of food, reducing logistical barriers.
  • Community support: We uplift marginalized communities by ensuring they have access to nutritious meals through community generosity.

üåç The Reality

Food waste is a major problem in India. According to the United Nations, India wastes over 68 million tons of food every year‚Äîfood that could feed the country's undernourished population several times over. At the same time, over 190 million people in India struggle with hunger or malnutrition daily.

The majority of food waste comes from households, restaurants, weddings, and large events. Often, this food is perfectly edible but discarded due to lack of awareness, poor planning, or absence of a reliable redistribution system.

Daawat was born to bridge that gap‚Äîto turn waste into nourishment, and indifference into action.
